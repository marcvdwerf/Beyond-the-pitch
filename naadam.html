The Legacy of the Nine White Banners

At the heart of the Naadam opening ceremony lies a tradition dating back to the Mongol Empire. You will witness the vibrant parade where mounted cavalry carry the Nine White Banners.

These banners, made from the tail hair of a thousand white stallions, were originally used by Chingis Khaan to symbolize peace and the unification of the Mongol tribes. Today, they remain the ultimate symbol of Mongolian statehood and national pride, brought from the Government Palace to the stadium only once a year to signal the start of the Great Festival.

Day 1: The Grand Opening

Day 2: The Steppe & Closing

  • 08:00 Drive 40km from Ulaanbaatar to the horse racing venue in the vast Mongolian Steppe.
  • Race Experience the energy of long-distance horse races, the pride of nomadic sport. Boxed lunch included.
  • Close Return to the stadium for the wrestling finals and the official closing ceremony of Naadam.
